The one and a half story farm house is located on the north side of Mountain Rest Road east of Canaan Road.  Constructed in c. 1800, the house has an entry porch with a modern dormer above and faces Mountain Rest Road. It is set back from Mountain Rest Road and is hidden behind large shrubs and mature trees. The outbuildings, including a Dutch barn, are located to the north and northwest of the house. A driveway runs between the house and a carriage barn to the north.

 

The Vanostrandt farm at 107 Mountain Rest Road once was bounded by Canaan Road on the west, Mountain Rest Road on the south and the Wurtz Farm (Buckeye Farm) at 33 Mountain Rest Road, to the east. The property once contained over 75 acres, but only 53.60 acres remains with the house. It appears to have comprised land from two large rectangular lots that had been created to divide land in this part of the town.  The eastern part of the parcel contains a large marsh.  There is cleared land around the farmstead, and woods in northern and western sections.  The historic property lines are still obvious on the attached aerial photograph and tax parcel map. A small parcel containing 2.09 acres was sold off in the 1960s and now contains a house which faces the wetland to the east.

 

 

4.     SIGNIFICANCE:           

The Vanostrandt family is the earliest recorded residents of the property. James Vanostrant is listed on the 1837 New Paltz Tax Rolls as having 79 acres with an assessed value of $1,100.  One of his sons, John J. Vanostrandt, purchased the farm across the road at 106 Mountain Rest Road and worked that farm until his father retired. Once John retired, John J. and his family moved back to his family farm at 107 Mountain Rest Road and took over farming there.  The 1850 census identified the property as a hotel suggesting that the Vanostrandts were taking in summer boarders at this time.

 

James Van Ostrandt appears in the town tax rolls in 1837, he could not be identified in the 1830 or 1840 censuses for New Paltz (or anywhere else).  James was 50 years of age in 1837; there were entries for younger Van Ostrandt men in New Paltz, named Frederick, Lewis and James, who were evidently relations.
