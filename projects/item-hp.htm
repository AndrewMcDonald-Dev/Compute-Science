250 White Duck Road

 

 

CONTENT:

One and a half story single family Federal home thought to be constructed around 1820 of brick; on a stone foundation, with brick veneer exterior and gable roof. The property is described as having 5 bay, center passage, endwall brick chimneys, tripartite horizontal attic windows within wide cornice, window shutters, 1-story side ell with attic & end chimney, front & rear porches, window & door headers with soldier course brickwork, Dutch doors and sidelights. A two story wood frame barn; on a stone foundation and cellar walkout, was added to the property in 1820. Open farm fields, wood paneled and fluted column entrance gate posts and attached swinging gates were observed during the 2004 survey. The property is also known as the "Wallkill River Farm".

* The date of construction on this property could not be verified by the existing historic record. The date of construction used is from the New York State Office of Real Property Services, and can not be verified as accurate.
